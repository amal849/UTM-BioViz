Muscle contraction mechanism. DataBase Center for Life Science (DBCLS), Mechanism of skeletal muscle contraction, 2021. Licensed under CC BY 4.0.

What is this?
This is a diagram of an actin-myosin cross-bridge cycle, which is used by muscle cells to undergo muscle contraction.
How do I understand it?
Step 1 - This diagram is read in a clockwise direction, beginning with the illustration at the top where the myosin head (in pink) is attached to part of the actin filament (purple/orange).
Step 2 - Analyze the meaning of each arrow. All the arrows in this figure have multiple meanings. For example, straight arrows show how one event causes the next, and curved arrows merging with straight arrows show the addition of a product. In this diagram, ATP comes in and attaches to the myosin head, causing it to detach from the myosin head.
Step 3 - Next, look at curved arrows diverging from straight arrows, which show the removal of a product. In this diagram, an inorganic phosphate molecule detaches from myosin, and ADP leaves soon after.
Step 4 - Then, observe the other smaller arrows in this diagram. For instance, the bold arrow pointing to the right represents the direction of actin filament movement. Similarly, the small curved arrow in the bottom right figure shows the movement of the myosin head as it is cocked back into position.
Quick Facts:
  • Curved arrows merging with a straight arrows shows how the addition of a product leads to the next event.
  • Curved arrows diverging from a straight arrow shows how the removal of a product leads to the next event.
Common Misconception:
"This process diagram is not really a process because there is no arrow between the bottom two figures."
You should still interpret the bottom two figures as one event leading to the next, even without an arrow. Although this is a process diagram, the arrows are not used effectively, and there should be an arrow between the two figures to show the continuation of the cycle.
